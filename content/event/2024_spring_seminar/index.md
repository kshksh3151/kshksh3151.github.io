summary: In house seminar about the physical foundation for simulations & basic instrumental analysis
abstract: 'In the perspective of simulating and analyzing various chemical phenomena for computational scientists, understanding the underlying physics of simulation tools and target phenomena is crucial for proceeding with better research. Especially, the importance of studying the physiochemical view of target phenomena is often underestimated by many graduate students. Sometimes, it becomes a serious bottleneck for troubleshooting in several research projects or proposing new ones. Also, for computational scientists, it is easy to overlook getting an experimentalist’s view or approaches in their research. However, to create better synergies in collaboration between experimentalists and computational scientists, acquiring a minimum understanding or language for experimental approaches is necessary. For that reason, this spring seminar was planned and will be proceeded.

This seminar consists of two main parts: physical foundations for various simulations and phenomena for Part I, and Basic Instrumental Analysis for Part II. In Part I, we will cover the underlying physics for several simulation tools or target phenomena of our group. Because we already covered the methodology-specific topics for basic simulation tools in the last seminar, we will mostly focus on the target phenomena of our laboratory. In Part II, elemental experimental knowledge for computational chemists will be the main theme. Particularly, the common principles of instrumental measurements, spectroscopic tools for soft matter research or electrochemical research, and the synergies with computational research to reveal the fundamentals of chemical phenomena will be several topics.
'

abstract: 'Controlling the morphology and enhancing the coupled motion of ion transport and electronic conduction in polymer-based organic electronics are key to designing next-generation energy conversion materials and bioelectronics. However, modeling the dynamics of molecules and electron transport using conventional computational methods (such as DFT or MD simulations) has inherent limitations due to highly correlated dynamics of molecules and electrons. In our study, we developed a hybrid Monte Carlo-Molecular Dynamics scheme to emulate the coupled dynamics of molecules and electrons with reduced computational costs. We applied the kinetics related to electron transport in the Monte Carlo(MC) simulations, and captured the atomistic motions of the constituent molecules in the MD simulations. By combining these simulations, we successfully reproduced the highly coupled electron dynamics and molecular mechanics of polymer-based organic electronics in low-cost nanoscale simulations.'
